One of the nice stats we can get from baseball savant is expected stats based on how hard and the launch angle of a player’s balls in play. Using these expected stats often helps show whether a player is struggling or just going through a rough stretch of variance. Here is a nice explainer of wOBA and xWOBA from MLB’s technology blog.

As an example, consider Ozzie Albie’s pedestrian slashline through 15 games and 54 at-bats so far in 2021 - .148/.262/.333/.595. His expected stats tell a bit of a different story though. His expected batting average (xBA) is .302 - more than double his actual batting average and a career high.
